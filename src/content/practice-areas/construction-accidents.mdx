## What You Need to Know

Every personal injury case is unique. The facts, the parties involved, the extent of your injuries, and the insurance coverage available all factor into what you may be entitled to recover. That's why it's critical to speak with an attorney before making any statements to an insurance company or accepting any settlement offers.

## What Compensation May Be Available

Depending on the specifics of your case, you may be entitled to recover:

- **Medical expenses** — current and future treatment costs, surgery, physical therapy, medications
- **Lost wages** — income you've lost while recovering, and reduced earning capacity if your injuries affect your future ability to work
- **Pain and suffering** — physical pain and emotional distress caused by the accident and its aftermath
- **Property damage** — repair or replacement of your vehicle or other property
- **Loss of consortium** — impact on your relationships and quality of life
- **Punitive damages** — in cases involving gross negligence or intentional misconduct

## Why You Need an Attorney

Insurance companies are not on your side. Their adjusters are trained to minimize payouts — and they start working against you from the moment the claim is filed. Without an experienced personal injury attorney advocating for you, you risk:

- Accepting a settlement that doesn't cover your future medical costs
- Making a recorded statement that can be used to reduce your claim
- Missing important deadlines that could eliminate your right to recover entirely
- Overlooking liable parties who could significantly increase your recovery
